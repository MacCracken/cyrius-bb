# Breakout release-date verification — action item

> **Status**: open. Verify before finalizing launch collateral.

## Context

cyrius-bb's v1.0 ship target is **2026-06-13 (Saturday)**, positioned as *"50 years + 2 months after Breakout's original release, same day-of-month."* That framing relies on Breakout's original US arcade release being **April 13, 1976** — the most commonly cited date across secondary sources.

But some Atari internal deployment records and historical accounts push the release into May 1976. Primary-source verification is cheap and worth doing before any launch collateral (article, press blurb, donation-bomb copy, tweet) pins the date.

## Sources to check (in rough credibility order)

1. **"Atari: Business Is Fun"** (Goldberg & Vendel, 2012) — the most-thorough published history of Atari, written with primary-source access. Should have the most precise shipping date available.
2. **Nolan Bushnell's interviews + podcasts** — he's been interviewed hundreds of times; a direct Bushnell statement on the shipping date carries primary-source weight.
3. **Steve Wozniak's "iWoz"** autobiography — Woz designed the board in four days and nights. His account references when Atari shipped it.
4. **The Killer List of Videogames (KLOV / arcade-museum.com)** — community-maintained, but usually primary-sourced; cross-check with Atari-insider sources.
5. **Atari Corporation archives** (held at the Computer History Museum, Mountain View, CA) — if digital catalog is accessible online, may have the cabinet-shipment log.
6. **Video Games and Computer Entertainment magazine retrospectives** — 1980s-90s industry press with primary-source interview access.

## Decision paths after verification

- **If April 13, 1976 is confirmed**: framing holds. *"50 years + 2 months, same day-of-month"* is the clean story.
- **If the actual date is May 13, 1976 or later**: either (a) shift cyrius-bb's v1.0 target to match the new anniversary-plus-interval, or (b) keep June 13 and reframe as *"50-year-era homage"* without the specific day-of-month echo. Coordinator's call.
- **If sources give conflicting dates**: pick the most-primary-source-credible and cite it in launch collateral; acknowledge the historical ambiguity in the knife article if relevant.
